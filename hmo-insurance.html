What is HMO Insurance?

HMO Insurance is specialist cover designed for landlords who rent out Houses in Multiple Occupation. Unlike standard landlord insurance, HMO policies provide protection against the unique risks of renting to multiple tenants under separate agreements, ensuring cover for property damage, liability claims, and loss of rent.

For professional landlords, property investors, and letting agents, HMO Insurance is essential to protect your property, income, and liabilities.

Who Needs HMO Insurance?

If you own or manage an HMO, specialist insurance is crucial to protect your investment. This includes:

  • HMO Landlords – Owners of shared houses, student accommodation, and multi-tenant properties.
  • Portfolio Landlords – Those managing multiple HMOs or rental properties.
  • Letting Agents – Managing HMOs on behalf of landlords.

Why is HMO Insurance Important?

Standard landlord insurance often doesn’t cover HMOs. Without specialist cover, you could be exposed to significant financial losses if something goes wrong.

Common risks covered by HMO Insurance include:

  • Property Damage – Protection against fire, flood, storms, and tenant-related damage.
  • Loss of Rent – Covers lost income if your property becomes uninhabitable due to an insured event.
  • Landlord Liability – Covers legal fees and compensation if a tenant or visitor is injured on your property.
  • Alternative Accommodation – Covers rehousing costs for tenants if the property is uninhabitable.

What’s Covered in HMO Insurance?

HMO Insurance policies typically include:

  • Buildings Insurance – Protection for structural damage and repairs.
  • Landlord Contents Insurance – Covers furnishings in common areas.
  • Landlord Liability – Protects against tenant injury claims.
  • Loss of Rent – Compensation for rental income loss due to insured events.
  • Malicious Damage Cover – Protection against intentional damage by tenants.

What’s Not Covered?

  • General wear and tear of the property.
  • Non-payment of rent (unless rent guarantee cover is included).
  • Tenant negligence (unless covered under an optional add-on).

Additional Cover You May Need

Depending on your HMO, you may require additional cover such as:

  • Rent Guarantee Insurance – Covers unpaid rent if tenants default.
  • Landlord Legal Expenses – Protection for legal costs in disputes with tenants.
  • Home Emergency Cover – 24/7 assistance for boiler breakdowns, plumbing issues, and more.
  • Multiple Property Insurance – Simplifies cover if you own multiple HMOs.

HMO Insurance: Frequently Asked Questions

What is HMO insurance?
HMO (House in Multiple Occupation) insurance is a specialist type of landlord insurance designed to cover rental properties occupied by multiple tenants who are not part of the same household. It typically provides protection for the building, landlord liability, loss of rent, and accidental damage.
What does HMO insurance cover?
HMO insurance typically covers:
  • Buildings insurance for structural damage
  • Landlord liability protection
  • Loss of rent coverage
  • Accidental and malicious damage by tenants
  • Legal expenses and eviction costs
  • Alternative accommodation costs
Do I legally need HMO insurance?
While HMO insurance is not a legal requirement, mortgage lenders may insist on it, and local licensing laws often require adequate landlord liability cover. Standard landlord insurance may not provide sufficient protection for an HMO property.
How much does HMO insurance cost?
The cost of HMO insurance varies depending on factors such as:
  • Property size and location
  • Number of tenants
  • Level of cover required
  • Property security measures
  • Previous claims history
It's advisable to compare quotes to find the best coverage for your needs.
What type of tenants does HMO insurance cover?
HMO insurance can cover a range of tenants, including:
  • Students
  • Working professionals
  • DSS tenants
  • Key workers
  • Housing benefit claimants (with select insurers)
Always check with your insurer to ensure the policy suits your tenant type.
Does HMO insurance include landlord liability cover?
Yes, most HMO insurance policies include landlord liability cover, protecting against claims from tenants or visitors who suffer injury or damage due to the property. Coverage typically starts at £2 million and can go up to £10 million.
