What is Accidental Damage Cover?

Accidental Damage Cover is an optional add-on to your home insurance policy that protects against unintentional damage caused by sudden accidents—like spilling paint on the carpet, smashing a TV, or cracking a bathroom sink.

It’s ideal for families, homeowners, and DIY enthusiasts who want extra peace of mind beyond standard home insurance protection.

Who Needs Accidental Damage Cover?

If you want to be covered for the unexpected, this type of insurance is worth considering. It’s particularly valuable for:

  • Busy households – Where life’s chaos can lead to accidental spills or breakages.
  • Families with children – Cover for unplanned mishaps like a cracked TV or juice on the sofa.
  • DIYers – Protection if a project goes wrong and causes damage to your home.
  • Pet owners – Cover for accidents caused by curious paws or wagging tails.

Why is Accidental Damage Cover Important?

Standard home insurance doesn’t always cover accidents. Without this additional protection, you could be left to pay out of pocket for costly repairs or replacements caused by mishaps.

Common types of accidental damage include:

  • Spilled drinks on carpets or electronics
  • Broken windows or mirrors
  • TVs knocked over
  • DIY accidents, like drilled pipes or damaged walls

What’s Typically Covered?

Accidental Damage Cover can apply to both buildings and contents, depending on your policy. Examples include:

  • Buildings Cover – Structural damage from DIY accidents or dropped items.
  • Contents Cover – Cover for items like TVs, laptops, sofas, and carpets damaged by accident.

What’s Not Covered?

  • General wear and tear or deterioration over time.
  • Damage caused deliberately or through negligence.
  • Pet damage (unless specifically included in your policy).
  • Damage while the home is left unoccupied beyond policy limits.

Additional Cover You May Need

To build a more complete safety net around your home, you may also want to consider:

  • Home Emergency Cover – For sudden issues like boiler breakdowns or leaks.
  • Personal Possessions Cover – Protect items taken outside the home, like phones and jewellery.
  • Family Legal Protection – Help with legal costs from home-related disputes or claims.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Accidental Damage Cover?
Accidental Damage Cover is an optional upgrade to your home insurance policy that protects against unexpected, one-off incidents like spilling paint, smashing a TV, or damaging your walls during DIY.
What does Accidental Damage Cover include?
It typically includes:
  • Spills on carpets or furniture.
  • Accidentally broken windows, TVs, or mirrors.
  • DIY mishaps, like drilled pipes or damaged fittings.
  • Dropped items causing damage to floors or worktops.
Coverage can apply to both buildings and contents, depending on your policy.
Is Accidental Damage Cover included as standard?
No, most home insurance policies do not include Accidental Damage Cover as standard. It’s usually available as an optional add-on that you can choose when setting up or renewing your policy.
What isn’t covered by Accidental Damage insurance?
Common exclusions include:
  • General wear and tear or gradual deterioration.
  • Deliberate or malicious damage.
  • Pet-related damage (unless specified).
  • Damage due to poor maintenance or neglect.
Always check your policy wording for exact exclusions.
Do I really need Accidental Damage Cover?
If you have children, pets, or do DIY at home, accidental damage is more likely. This cover can save you from unexpected costs and give you peace of mind that life’s small accidents won’t lead to big bills.
